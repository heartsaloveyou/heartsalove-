About “Hearts a Love”

A finite universe of women’s names and abstract hearts, researched and created by super class 7.

We started with names, not with hype. heartsalove.com maps thousands of women’s names from Western, Asian and African cultures, keeping their original spellings. From that research, we built a finite universe of around five thousand names — a tiny, poetic fraction of the millions of women who carry them.

Each name receives an abstract heart created by the same hand inside the studio super class 7. These hearts are not personality tests. They are free visual interpretations that share a name, not definitions of a person. The real meaning comes from the stories people attach to them: why this name, this heart, this moment.

The collection unfolds in NFT drops on Magic Eden, and each heart can be claimed and printed in large format. Over time, the public’s own reasons for choosing names become part of the artwork itself.

Key points

• ~5,000 women’s names across Western, Asian and African cultures.
• Each name paired with a unique abstract heart, created by one artist’s hand.

“Mary Jean” × Doctors Without Borders (MSF)

One heart that carries a different emotional weight, opened to support real women in real crises.

Within this universe, “Mary Jean” is a singular heart. It carries the birth name of Marilyn Monroe — a woman whose image was adored while her pain was often ignored. Inside our studio, this was the work that demanded the most time, attention and emotional weight. The same hand that shaped every heart in the collection spent longer here, layer after layer, until the image felt heavy enough to stand alone.

All proceeds from the auction of “Mary Jean” go to Doctors Without Borders / Médecins Sans Frontières (MSF), supporting medical work in real crises far away from any glamour. The donation will be sent through an official MSF channel, and the transaction will be publicly verifiable so collectors can follow the path from artwork to aid.

Reserve price: 1,000 USD equivalent. The auction runs in parallel with the early drops of “Corações femininos / Hearts a Love”.
